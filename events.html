What We've Been Up To

Hanging out with the community to spread some hope.

Community Outreach

Leander Diwali Festival

We had a blast at the Leander Diwali Festival! Over 15,000 people were there, so it was a huge chance for us to connect with the community.

We spent the day handing out flyers and talking to people at our booth. We even got to go on stage and tell everyone about our mission, which was super cool to see so much support!

Education & Support

School Peer Lesson

We worked with our school therapist to run a Peer Lesson for students. We talked about real stuff like finding school resources, practical ways to support friends who are struggling, and building a community that actually cares.

It was a great chance to open up the conversation and give everyone the tools to help themselves and each other.

Awareness Campaign

Library Awareness Presentation

From December 10th to 12th, we set up a big info hub right in the school library. Our presentation was front and center, so everyone saw it during their daily routine.

We covered risk factors, prevention, and how to spot when a friend is struggling. Plus, we handed out info cards and stickers to get people talking in a safe space.

Community Fundraising

Bake Sale Fundraiser

We did a bake sale at Veteran's Memorial Park, and it was awesome to see so many people come out to support us.

Thanks to you guys, we raised over $140! We donated all of it to the JED Foundation to help them do their incredible work preventing suicide for teens and young adults.

Expert Insight

Guest Speaker Seminar

We were super lucky to have Mr. Malfatti come talk to us. He used to work in Drug Court, so he really knows his stuff when it comes to risk factors and addiction.

He shared some personal stories that were really powerful and honest. It wasn't just a lecture; it was a real conversation that everyone learned from.

School Community

Affirmation Board

We made this "Affirmation Board" for the club fair, and it was so cool to see how many people got involved. We just put out some markers and sticky notes, and soon the whole thing was covered in positive messages.

It was a simple way to show that everyone matters. Seeing people stop to read a nice note or write one for someone else was a great reminder of how supportive our school can be.

Education & Awareness

Neurologist Interview

We got to interview Dr. Peckham, a neurologist, and ask her all about the science behind addiction. She explained how substance use actually changes the brain, which was honestly mind-blowing.

It was super interesting to hear an expert break it down. Dr. Peckham made the science easy to understand and really helped us see why prevention is so important.

Community Action

Spirit Week

We organized a "Spirit Week" to get everyone talking about substance use prevention in a fun way. We had themed days and activities that got the whole school involved.

It was awesome to see so many students participating and showing their support for a drug-free lifestyle. Plus, it was just a really fun week for everyone.
